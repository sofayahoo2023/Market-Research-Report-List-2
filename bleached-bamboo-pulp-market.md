<p><p>The bleached bamboo pulp market encompasses two main types of production processes: the sulfate process and the caustic soda process. The sulfate process utilizes chemical reactions with sulfate as a primary agent to break down the bamboo fibers and obtain high-quality pulp. On the other hand, the caustic soda process involves the use of caustic soda and heat to dissolve the bamboo components, resulting in bleached pulp. Both methods provide different characteristics and qualities of pulp, catering to diverse market demands and applications.</p></p>
