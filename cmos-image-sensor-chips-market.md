<p><p>CMOS image sensor chips are crucial components used in digital cameras and other imaging devices. Front-illuminated image sensors have been around for a long time and consist of a layer of photodiodes placed on top of a circuitry layer, making them affordable but less sensitive to light. In contrast, backside-illuminated image sensors are designed with the light-sensitive layer behind the circuitry, enabling better light capture and improved performance in low-light conditions. They are more expensive to produce but offer higher image quality.</p></p>
