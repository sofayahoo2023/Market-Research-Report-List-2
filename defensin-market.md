<p><p>Defensins are small, cationic peptides involved in the innate immune response of humans. There are two main types: α-defensins and β-defensins. α-Defensins are predominantly found in neutrophils and are able to kill microbes directly by disrupting their membranes. β-Defensins, on the other hand, are more widely distributed in various tissues and can combat microbes through different mechanisms, including modulation of the immune response. These different types of defensins have potential applications in the development of antimicrobial therapies and diagnostics.</p></p>
