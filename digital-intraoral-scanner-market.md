<p><p>A digital intraoral scanner is a device used in dentistry to digitally capture images of teeth and the surrounding oral structures. It replaces the traditional method of taking physical impressions with a more accurate and efficient digital process. The scanner consists of a handheld wand-like device that captures images and transmits them to a computer for further analysis and treatment planning.</p><p>The market for digital intraoral scanners has witnessed significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue growing at a CAGR of 4.7% during the forecast period. One of the key drivers of market growth is the increasing adoption of digital solutions in dentistry. The benefits of digital intraoral scanners, such as improved accuracy, time savings, and enhanced patient comfort, are driving their popularity among dental practitioners.</p><p>Additionally, the rising prevalence of dental disorders and the growing demand for cosmetic dentistry are also contributing to market growth. The accuracy and efficiency of digital intraoral scanners make them ideal for dental procedures such as crowns, bridges, orthodontics, and implant placements.</p><p>A notable trend in the market is the integration of artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning algorithms in digital intraoral scanners. <p><p>The Digital Intraoral Scanner market is divided into two types: White Light and Laser. White Light scanners use a projecting light pattern and a camera to capture images. They are generally more affordable and have a higher accuracy level. On the other hand, Laser scanners emit a laser beam and capture images using a sensor. They are known for their faster scanning speed and ability to capture detailed images even in the presence of obstacles. Both types offer advanced digitization of oral impressions for dental applications.</p></p>
