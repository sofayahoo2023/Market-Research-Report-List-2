<p><p>Distributed Antenna Systems (DAS) are wireless communication networks that improve cellular coverage and capacity within a limited area. Active DAS utilizes electronic components to amplify and distribute signals, providing strong coverage in large venues. Passive DAS uses passive components, like coaxial cables, to distribute signals without amplification, making it cost-effective for smaller areas. Hybrid DAS combines both active and passive components, making it ideal for providing coverage in various environments. These different types of DAS cater to different coverage needs and offer flexibility and enhanced connectivity in specific locations.</p></p>
