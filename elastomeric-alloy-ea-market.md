<p><p>The elastomeric alloy (EA) market encompasses various types of alloys, including copper-based alloys, Fe-Ni-Cr alloys, Ni-Ct alloys, Ni-Cr-Nb alloys, Ni-Co-Cr alloys, and Nb-Ti alloys. Copper-based alloys consist mainly of copper and can possess excellent electrical conductivity and high corrosion resistance. Fe-Ni-Cr alloys, composed of iron, nickel, and chromium, offer good strength and oxidation resistance. Ni-Ct alloys are nickel-based and contain a small amount of cobalt and tantalum, providing resistance to high temperatures. Ni-Cr-Nb alloys contain nickel, chromium, and niobium, offering excellent corrosion resistance and thermal stability. Ni-Co-Cr alloys combine nickel, cobalt, and chromium, possessing exceptional strength and resistance to wear. Nb-Ti alloys, composed of niobium and titanium, possess high strength, corrosion resistance, and superconductivity properties.</p></p>
