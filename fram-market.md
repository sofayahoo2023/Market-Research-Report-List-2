<p><p>FRAM stands for Ferroelectric Random Access Memory, which is a type of non-volatile memory technology that utilizes the properties of ferroelectric materials to store data. This growth can be attributed to several factors. Firstly, the increasing demand for low-power, high-speed, and non-volatile memory solutions is driving the adoption of FRAM in various applications such as smart cards, wearable devices, automotive systems, and Internet of Things (IoT) devices. The ability of FRAM to provide low power consumption, high endurance, and fast write speeds makes it an attractive choice for these applications.</p><p>Moreover, the rising demand for data storage in emerging technologies such as artificial intelligence, machine learning, and autonomous vehicles is further fueling the market growth. FRAM's ability to provide fast and reliable data storage with minimal data loss in case of power failures is a key advantage in these applications.</p><p>Additionally, advancements in manufacturing technologies and the increasing number of product launches by key players in the FRAM market are contributing to its growth.
