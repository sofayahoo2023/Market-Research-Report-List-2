<p><p>Hepatocyte Growth Factor (HGF) is a paracrine cellular growth and motility factor that plays a crucial role in the development and regeneration of tissues. It is primarily synthesized and secreted by mesenchymal cells, such as fibroblasts and endothelial cells, and acts on hepatocytes through the c-Met receptor. HGF activates various signaling pathways, including those involved in cell proliferation, survival, migration, invasion, and angiogenesis. As a result, it has potential therapeutic applications in treating various diseases, including liver regeneration, tissue repair, and cancer therapy.</p><p>The Hepatocyte Growth Factor Market is witnessing significant growth and is expected to continue growing at a CAGR of 5.1% during the forecast period. Pharmaceutical companies are actively involved in the development of HGF analogs, antibodies, and small molecule inhibitors to target c-Met receptor and inhibit HGF signaling, thereby suppressing tumor growth, angiogenesis, and metastasis.</p><p>Moreover, there is an increasing focus on exploring the potential of HGF as a therapeutic agent for liver regeneration and tissue repair.
