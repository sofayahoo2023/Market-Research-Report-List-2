<p><p>Hyperosmotic laxatives are a type of laxative that works by drawing water into the bowel, resulting in increased bowel movements. There are three types of hyperosmotic laxatives on the market: lactulose, polymer, and saline. Lactulose type laxatives contain lactulose as the active ingredient, which is a synthetic sugar that promotes water absorption in the bowel. Polymer type laxatives contain polyethylene glycol or sorbitol as the active ingredient, which also increase water absorption. Saline type laxatives contain magnesium salts or sodium phosphate that draw water into the bowel through osmosis.</p></p>
