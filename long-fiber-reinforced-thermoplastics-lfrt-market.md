<p><p>Long Fiber Reinforced Thermoplastics (LFRT) are composite materials that consist of thermoplastic polymers reinforced with long fibers, typically ranging from 3 to 25 millimeters in length. These fibers can be PAN-based or pitch-based. PAN-based fibers are made from a synthetic polymer derived from acrylonitrile, which provides high strength and stiffness to the composite. On the other hand, pitch-based fibers are derived from coal or petroleum pitch, offering excellent thermal and chemical resistance. Both types of fibers are commonly used in various industries, including automotive, aerospace, and construction, due to their enhanced mechanical properties and lightweight nature.</p></p>
