<p><p>Microencapsulation is a technique used to encapsulate active compounds within tiny particles, known as microcapsules. These microcapsules can be made from different types of materials depending on the desired properties and applications. Polymers, such as polyurethane, are commonly used due to their versatility and ability to control release rates. Gums and resins, like gum arabic or shellac, provide good stability and have high solubility. Lipids, such as oils and fats, are often used for their barrier properties and compatibility with oil-based substances. Carbohydrates, like starch or dextrin, are biodegradable and offer good encapsulation efficiency. Proteins, like gelatin or whey protein, have excellent biocompatibility and can be used for controlled release applications.</p></p>
