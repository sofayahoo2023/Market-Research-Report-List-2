<p><p>Modified flour refers to a type of flour that has been modified either chemically or physically to alter its characteristics and improve its functionality in various food applications. These modifications are done to optimize the flour for specific food products and meet the demands of the food industry.</p><p>The modified flour market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. One of the key factors driving this growth is the rising demand for convenience food products such as baked goods, snacks, and processed foods. Modified flour enhances the texture, taste, and shelf life of these products, making them more appealing to consumers.</p><p>Additionally, the increasing health consciousness among consumers is also fueling the demand for modified flour. Modified flour can be fortified with vitamins, minerals, and fiber, making it a healthier alternative to regular flour. The demand for fortified and functional foods is on the rise, and modified flour manufacturers are tapping into this opportunity by offering specialized products that cater to specific dietary requirements.</p><p>Furthermore, the growing trend of gluten-free and low-carb diets is driving the demand for modified flour. Modified flours can be used as substitutes for traditional wheat flour in gluten-free products, providing similar texture and taste. This has led to an increased adoption of modified flours in the production of gluten-free baked goods, pasta, and other food items.</p><p>In conclusion, the modified flour market is poised for significant growth, driven by factors such as the increasing demand for convenience foods, rising health consciousness, and the trend of gluten-free diets.
