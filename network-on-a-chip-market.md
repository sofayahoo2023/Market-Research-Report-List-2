<p><strong>The Network on a Chip Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Direct Topology</li><li>Indirect Topology</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Network on a Chip (NoC) market can be classified into two main types: Direct Topology and Indirect Topology. </p><p>In the Direct Topology market, the NoC architecture is designed with a direct connection between the network nodes, allowing for efficient and fast communication. This direct connection enables a high level of scalability and lower power consumption, making it suitable for applications that require high-performance computing.</p><p>In contrast, the Indirect Topology market involves a more complex network structure with multiple interconnected nodes. This indirect connection offers better flexibility, fault tolerance, and adaptability to system changes. Indirect Topology NoCs are typically used in larger and more intricate systems, such as data centers or cloud computing, where the focus is on optimizing resource utilization and managing data flow efficiently.</p></p>
