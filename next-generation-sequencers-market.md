<p><p>Next Generation Sequencers (NGS) refer to a group of advanced technologies used to sequence DNA or RNA molecules. The market types within NGS include Sequencing by Synthesis (SBS), which utilizes synchronized cycles of synthesis and imaging; Ion Semiconductor Sequencing, whereby nucleotides release hydrogen ions during DNA synthesis; and Single-Molecule Real-Time (SMRT) Sequencing, which observes the DNA polymerase activity in real-time. Furthermore, other NGS technologies encompass diverse approaches, such as nanopore sequencing or DNA sequencing based on optical detection. These market types of NGS offer varying benefits and capabilities for DNA sequencing applications in research, diagnostics, and personalized medicine.</p></p>
