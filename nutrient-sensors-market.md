<p><p>Nutrient sensors are devices used to determine the levels of various nutrients in water or other solutions. These sensors can detect different types of nutrients such as nitrate, nitrite, ammonia, phosphate, and other nutrients. Nitrate sensors measure the concentration of nitrate ions, which are important indicators of water pollution. Nitrite sensors measure the levels of nitrite ions, which can indicate the presence of harmful bacteria. Ammonia sensors detect the levels of ammonia, which is a common pollutant in water bodies. Phosphate sensors measure the concentration of phosphate ions, which are essential nutrients but can also cause water pollution if present in excess. Other nutrient sensors can detect additional nutrients like potassium, calcium, and magnesium.</p></p>
