<p><p>Optical reflector film is classified into two main types: non-metallic films and metallic films. Non-metallic films are made from materials such as polyesters or polymers and have a transparent or translucent appearance. They are commonly used in applications where a high level of reflectivity is required, such as in lighting fixtures or display screens. On the other hand, metallic films are made from thin layers of metals such as aluminum or silver. They offer a higher level of reflectivity compared to non-metallic films and find applications in areas like solar panels or architectural glass coatings.</p></p>
