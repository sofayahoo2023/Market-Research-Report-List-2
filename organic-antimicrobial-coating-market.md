<p><p>The organic antimicrobial coating market can be classified into two main types: low molecular and macromolecule coatings. Low molecular coatings consist of small compounds that have antimicrobial properties. These coatings are typically applied on surfaces to inhibit the growth of microorganisms. On the other hand, macromolecule coatings are composed of larger molecules that form a protective layer on surfaces, preventing the attachment and growth of microbes. Both types of coatings provide effective antimicrobial protection, but they differ in terms of their molecular size and composition.</p></p>
