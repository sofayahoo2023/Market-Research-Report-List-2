<p><p>Photovoltaic devices, also known as solar cells, are devices that convert sunlight directly into electricity. These devices utilize the photovoltaic effect, which occurs when certain materials exhibit a property of producing an electric current when exposed to sunlight. The electricity generated by photovoltaic devices can be used to power various electronic devices or can be fed back into the grid.</p><p>The photovoltaic devices market has witnessed significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue growing at a healthy rate during the forecast period. The increasing demand for clean and sustainable energy sources, coupled with the declining prices of photovoltaic systems, is driving the market growth. Additionally, government initiatives and incentives to promote renewable energy adoption are further contributing to the market expansion.</p><p>The market is also witnessing various trends that are shaping its growth trajectory. One of the significant trends is the integration of photovoltaic devices into building structures, known as building integrated photovoltaics (BIPV). This integration allows for the generation of electricity while also serving as a design element in the construction industry.</p><p>Another trend in the market is the emergence of thin-film photovoltaic technology, which offers flexibility and versatility in terms of design and application. Thin-film solar cells are lightweight and can be easily integrated into various surfaces, including curved or flexible structures.</p><p>Furthermore, research and development efforts are focused on improving the efficiency of photovoltaic devices and reducing the cost per watt of electricity generation. This trend is driving advancements in materials, manufacturing processes, and cell designs.</p><p>Overall, the photovoltaic devices market is poised for substantial growth in the coming years, driven by the increasing demand for clean energy and ongoing technological advancements. The market is projected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 14.1% during the forecast period.</p></p>
