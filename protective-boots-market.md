<p><p>Protective boots are available in two primary market types: high-top and low-cut. High-top boots typically extend above the ankle, providing enhanced stability and protection to the wearer's feet and lower legs. These boots are commonly preferred in hazardous work environments such as construction sites, where the risk of ankle injuries is higher. On the other hand, low-cut boots are designed to cover only the foot, providing flexibility and comfort while still offering protection. These boots find application in industries where agility and ease of movement are crucial, such as hospitality or healthcare sectors.</p></p>
