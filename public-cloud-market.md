<p><p>Public cloud refers to the delivery of computing resources, such as storage, virtual machines, and applications, over the internet by third-party providers. It is a multi-tenant environment where multiple organizations share the same infrastructure while maintaining their own isolation. Public cloud enables businesses to access and utilize computing resources on-demand, allowing flexibility, scalability, and cost-efficiency.</p><p>The public cloud market has witnessed significant growth over the past few years and is expected to continue its upward trajectory in the coming years. Factors driving the market growth include the increasing adoption of cloud-based services by organizations across various industries, the rising demand for cost-effective IT infrastructure, and the need for faster deployment and scalability. The COVID-19 pandemic has also accelerated the adoption of public cloud solutions, as remote work and online services became crucial for business continuity.</p><p>Moreover, the market is benefiting from the growing digital transformation initiatives and the shift from traditional on-premises IT infrastructure to cloud-based solutions. The public cloud market is witnessing several notable trends, including the increasing adoption of hybrid cloud models that combine public and private clouds for enhanced flexibility and data security. Additionally, the market is witnessing a surge in the adoption of serverless computing, containerization, and artificial intelligence to further enhance efficiency and innovation.</p><p>According to market research, the global public cloud market is expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 3.70% during the forecast period. This growth can be attributed to the increasing demand for cloud-based infrastructure and services, the continued advancements in technology, and the expanding presence of major cloud service providers in the market. The market is anticipated to witness significant opportunities in various sectors, including healthcare, finance, e-commerce, and government, further driving its growth.</p></p>
