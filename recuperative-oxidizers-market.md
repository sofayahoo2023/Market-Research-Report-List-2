<p><p>Recuperative oxidizers are air pollution control devices used to remove volatile organic compounds (VOCs) and hazardous air pollutants (HAPs) from industrial exhaust streams. There are two main types of recuperative oxidizers: thermal and catalytic. </p><p>Thermal oxidizers use high temperatures to break down VOCs and HAPs into harmless byproducts. They are effective for treating large volumes of exhaust gas, but can be energy-intensive.</p><p>Catalytic oxidizers employ catalysts to facilitate the chemical reactions that convert VOCs and HAPs into less harmful substances. They operate at lower temperatures and require less energy compared to thermal oxidizers.</p></p>
