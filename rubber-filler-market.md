<p><p>The rubber filler market consists of two types: organic filler and inorganic filler. Organic fillers are derived from natural sources, such as plant fibers, wood flour, and cornstarch. They are environmentally friendly and add strength and flexibility to rubber products. In contrast, inorganic fillers are derived from non-living materials like minerals, metals, and ceramics. They enhance the physical properties of rubber, such as hardness, wear resistance, and heat resistance. Both types of fillers contribute to improving the performance and durability of rubber products.</p></p>
