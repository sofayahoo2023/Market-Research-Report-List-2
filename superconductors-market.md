<p><p>Superconductors are materials that exhibit zero electrical resistance when cooled below a certain critical temperature. The market for superconductors can be divided into two types: Low Temperature Superconductors (LTS) and High Temperature Superconductors (HTS). LTS are superconductors that operate at extremely low temperatures, typically below 30 Kelvin (-243.2°C). On the other hand, HTS can maintain superconductivity at relatively higher temperatures, above the boiling point of liquid nitrogen (-196°C). These different types of superconductors have distinct applications and characteristics, leading to various market opportunities and potential advancements in the field of technology.</p></p>
