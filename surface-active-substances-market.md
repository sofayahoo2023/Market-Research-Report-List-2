<p><p>Surface-active substances, also known as surfactants, are chemical compounds that lower the surface tension between liquids or between a liquid and a solid. They find applications in various industries like detergents, personal care products, textiles, and pharmaceuticals. Anionic surfactants are negatively charged and are commonly found in cleaning products. Non-ionic surfactants have no charge and are versatile, often used in agricultural and industrial formulations. Cationic surfactants are positively charged and primarily used as fabric softeners and disinfectants. Amphoteric surfactants can be either positively or negatively charged and are used in shampoos and conditioners. Other types of surfactants exist but are less commonly used.</p></p>
