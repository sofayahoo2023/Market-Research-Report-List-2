<p><p>Organic pigments are derived from carbon-based compounds and are usually made from coal tar or petroleum. They offer a wide range of vibrant colors and are often used in applications like paints, inks, plastics, and textiles. In contrast, inorganic pigments are made from minerals and metallic compounds, such as iron oxide, titanium dioxide, and cadmium sulfide. These pigments provide excellent stability, lightfastness, and resistance to heat, making them suitable for uses in coatings, ceramics, construction materials, and cosmetics. Both organic and inorganic pigments play crucial roles in various industries, featuring distinct properties and applications.</p></p>
